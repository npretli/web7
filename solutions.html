Web GIS Solution #2: ESRI Configurables (ArcGIS Dashboards)

See a walk-through below for how to configure your own ArcGIS Dashboard!

Step 1: Open your ArcGIS Pro Project / Workspace.

Step 2: Note the name of your geodatabase in the Catalog Pane.

Step 3: From File Explorer, zip the contents of your geodatabase folder.

Step 4: Sign in to ArcGIS Online.

Step 5: Under the content pane, select "Add Item", followed by "From your computer"

Step 6: Under File, navigate to the location where your geodatabase is stored on your personal PC. Under Contents, select File Geodatabase, and provide your geodatabase a title. Specify a tag.

Step 7: Select on the map thumbnail to open the web map.

Step 8: In this environment, the symbology and feature class titles will first need to be modified prior to the creation of the dashboard itself.

First, begin by clicking on a feature class of choice in the Contents pane - in this scenario, we'll begin with symbology (3rd symbol from the left).

Step 9: Select an attribute of interest, followed by a drawing style of interest.

Step 10: Select "Classify Data"

Step 11: Select the number of classes to be rendered.

Then, click on "Legend"

Step 12: Specify and edit the value ranges along the colour ramp as necessary (the colour ramp itself can also be modified from under "Symbols").

Step 13: Within the web map: select Basemap, and under the drop-down menu, select "Dark Gray Canvas" (or other basemap of choice)

Step 14: Note the name of your geodatabase in the Catalog Pane.

Step 34: Note the name of your geodatabase in the Catalog Pane.

Step 35: Select the blue ribbon on the top left of the map, and in the drop-down menu, select the wrench ("Configure")

Step 36: Select the "Map actions" pane.

Click the "Add target" drop-down menu, and add desired targets.

Step 37: Once the desired targets have been populated, click Done.

Step 38: Select "Header"

Step 39: Add a title & subtitle. Swap the text colour and background colour if necessary.

Step 40: Add any necessary supplementary information under "Description"

Step 41: Be sure to save your work.
